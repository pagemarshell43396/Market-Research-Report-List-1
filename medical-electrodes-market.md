<p><p>The medical electrodes market is categorized into disposable and reusable electrodes. Disposable electrodes are designed for single-use, offering convenience and reducing the risk of cross-contamination, commonly used in procedures like ECG and EEG. In contrast, reusable electrodes are durable, designed for multiple applications after proper cleaning and sterilization, often utilized in long-term monitoring settings. The choice between the two types typically hinges on cost-effectiveness, environmental considerations, and specific clinical requirements, influencing their application in healthcare settings.</p></p>
